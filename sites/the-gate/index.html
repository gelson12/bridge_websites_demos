Our Story

Where the gate stood, the pub stayed

The Gate has been pouring pints on this corner since 1876 — the year Victoria was Empress of India and the first telephone rang. Back then it was a coaching inn for travellers passing through the old tollgate. The gate is long gone, but the pub never left.

Six generations of landlords have kept the same spirit alive: a warm welcome, a well-kept pint, and a place where everybody knows your name — or will by the time you finish your first drink. The oak beams are original. The fires have never gone out. And the stories told across this bar could fill a library.

Today we're still family-run, still pouring local ales, and still the place the neighbourhood calls its second home. Pull up a stool.

— The Clarke family, licensees since 1963
