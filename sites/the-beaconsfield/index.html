Years of Hospitality

More than a pub. A home from home.

Tucked away on Green Lanes since the reign of Queen Victoria, The Beaconsfield has been the heart of the Stroud Green community for over a century and a half. The original oak bar still stands — polished smooth by generations of elbows, laughter, and quiet pints.

We're proudly independent, proudly unfussy. Expect a rotating lineup of impeccably kept cask ales, a wine list curated with love rather than margins, and a menu that celebrates British seasonal cooking — from Sunday roasts that draw queues round the corner to weeknight suppers that taste like someone actually cares.

No gimmicks. No gastropub pricing dressed up as fine dining. Just good company, proper hospitality, and the simple pleasure of a pub that knows exactly what it is.

Landlords Tom & Alice, since 2014

What We're Known For

From the perfect pint to the kind of Sunday lunch that feels like a hug.

Proper Pints, Proper Care

Twelve rotating cask ales and craft keg lines, poured with reverence. Our cellar is a climate-controlled sanctuary — the beer leaves the way it arrived, not a bubble out of place.

Seasonal British Fare

Our Head Chef sources from growers within a forty-mile radius. The menu changes with what's good — wild garlic in spring, game in autumn, and a sticky toffee pudding that has ended friendships over the last spoonful.

Your Local Community Hub

Quiz nights, a book club that actually reads the book, live folk every other Thursday, and a roaring fireplace that claims more guests than any of our rooms. We're a third place — not home, not work, but somewhere better.
