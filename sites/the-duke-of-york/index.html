Our StoryA Local With a Legacy

Tucked away on Uxbridge Road since the reign of Queen Victoria, The Duke of York has weathered wars, watched the trams roll by, and remained the steadfast heart of Hanwell. Our oak-panelled walls hold centuries of laughter, the glow of the fireplace has warmed generations, and our hand-pulled ales have quenched the thirst of every local from every walk of life.

Today, we honour that legacy: proper British hospitality, a seasonally-rotating menu built from British produce, one of West London's finest selections of cask ale and craft beer, and the same open-armed warmth that made us a landmark in 1847.
