What We Offer

Real Ale, Real Food, Real Warmth

From the first pull of the pump to the last crumb of Sunday roast — everything we do celebrates British pub culture at its finest.

Proper Cask Ale

Three ever-rotating cask ales from Britain's finest independent breweries, pulled the old-fashioned way. Always cellared at precisely 12°C, served with a head you can write your name in.

Hearty Home Cooking

Sunday roasts that draw queues down Stroud Green Road. Hand-carved meats, Yorkshire puddings the size of your fist, and gravy that's been reducing since breakfast. Lunch & dinner served daily.

The Heart of the Community

Quiz nights on Tuesdays, live folk on Sundays, and a roaring fireplace all winter. A place where locals know your name, your dog, and your usual order. Everyone's welcome at our hearth.

Our Story

A London Landmark Since Victorian Times

The White Lion of Mortimer has stood at 125–127 Stroud Green Road since 1879, pouring pints for generations of Londoners. Named after the Mortimer family crest — three lions passant guardant — this corner pub has weathered two world wars, seven monarchs, and countless North London rain showers.

Today, we're still family-run, still independent, and still dedicated to the simple art of the great British pub: fine cask ale, honest food, and a fireside welcome that never goes out of style.

"Pull up a stool — you're home."
