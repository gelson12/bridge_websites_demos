Founded in the spirit of discovery

The Linnean Society of London was established in 1788 to honor the legacy of Carl Linnaeus and to promote the study of natural history. Our archives contain the original manuscripts of Charles Darwin, Alfred Russel Wallace, and other pioneering scientists whose work fundamentally shaped modern biology.

Today, we continue as a learned society dedicated to the advancement of taxonomy, evolutionary biology, and the curation of natural history records. Our library is open to scholars and researchers worldwide, offering access to a unique and irreplaceable heritage of scientific inquiry.

Membership is by invitation and election, reflecting our commitment to scholarly excellence and sustained contribution to the field.
